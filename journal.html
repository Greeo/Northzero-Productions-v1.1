The Integrity of Lived Experience

In my time serving on the Historic Preservation Commission, the core challenge is always the same: how do we protect the soul of a structure while adapting it for the future? We’ve found that the same principle applies to Service Design and Advocacy. When we capture the "lived experience" of a youth in the foster system or a family facing housing insecurity, we aren't just recording a testimonial. We are preserving a human legacy. If that story is handled without structural care, its impact is lost in the bureaucracy of policy.

Engineering the Bridge

At Northzero, our "Structural Cinema" approach treats a narrative like a blueprint. We audit the foundation: The Context: What is the historical and systemic weight of this story? The Objective: How must this narrative pivot the listener’s perspective? The Execution: How do we use cinematic precision—light, sound, and pacing—to ensure the dignity of the subject remains unshakeable? When storytelling is engineered with this level of rigour, it stops being "content" and starts being a catalyst for systemic change.
